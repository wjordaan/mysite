Data Analysis Web App

Utilizing data gathered through 3rd party Electronic Drilling Recorder (EDR) systems, an interactive 3D representation of the wellbore is plotted. Allowing mapping of various drilling parameters such as VSS, WOB, ROP, Torque etc. to be analyzed to better understand drilling dynamics across multiple wellbores. Formation layers, run markers, and sub plots are utilized to further clarify drilling performance.

Parameters can be mapped to either colour or size to showcase severity, combined with its 3D geometric representation, multiple parameter relationships can be assessed not easily done using 2D methods. Such as wellbore deviation in the lateral or high local doglegs.

All data processing is self contained in web application, utilizing either an SQL database connection or raw CSV data file exported from EDR. Application rendering managed utilizing industry standard HTML and JavaScript allowing access on any machine connected to server using any modern web browser.

EDR Analysis

Data mining and analysis of dozens of wells to optimize survey points and minimize NPT. Analysis of data was done to determine time for each function performed while drilling, including off-bottom activities. Focus of project was around the impact of survey times, optimizing procedures and tool selection to minimize non-productive time (NPT). A comparative study of competitors was conducted, to identify areas of improvement along with areas of strength.

Study showed significant opportunity to reduce operator NPT, with icremental approach taken to apply lessons learned to optimize wellsite performance. Lessons learned included leveraging experience from other geomarkets, specifically US Land, to introduce alternative drilling solutions. Final results of study allowed the operator to reduce NPT by up to 18 hours per well.

Viking RNS

Optimal wellbore placement is crucial in maximizing the ultimate recovery from the reservoir. Traditional techniques follow a well plan based on predetermined geological models. Project examined heterogeneity of Viking formation, specifically with finding a sweet spot for wellbore placement to maximize ultimate recovery from reservoir. To achieve this reservoir navigation (RNS) was utilized. As traditional gamma ray has a very limited depth of investigation, deep azimuthal resistivity was required.

Working closely with clients, multiple fields were analyzed and compared to offset traditional wellbores in the area. Leveraging the deep azimuthal resistivity technology for reservoir navigation showed very positive results for improved wellbore placement. Resulting in increased production, with very minor increases in authority of expenditure (AFE).

RoqSCAN

RoqSCAN is a wellsite mineralogical analyzer. Developed for use at the wellsite, as well as in field offices and laboratories. The RoqSCAN analyzer delivers detailed petrographic analysis using drill cuttings, thin sections, and cores. The service combines powerful formation evaluation capabilities with customized software to measure total organic content (TOC) and source rock mineralogy, including type, clay species, elemental data, matrix density, brittleness, and ductility.

Spearheaded operations for launch into Canadian marketplace. Meeting with clients to detail the technology and showcasing applications for utilizing the data to design a comprehensive completion plan to increase production. Outside of sales, also provided supply chain logistics and managed operations for processing samples and compiling final deliverable data prior to client delivery.
